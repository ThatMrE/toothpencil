How It Works ✏️

It's a toothbrush. Shaped like a pencil. Made entirely from plants. That's it. That's the product.

1

Unbox Your Pencil

Arrives in a kraft paper box (recyclable, obviously). Pull it out like a No. 2 on test day.

2

Brush With It

Charcoal-infused bristles whiten naturally. Cedar handle feels warm in your hand. Hexagonal grip means no rolling off the counter.

3

Compost It

When you're done (every 3 months, dentist's orders), toss it in the compost. It becomes soil. A tree grows. Circle of life.

The General Pencil Story ✏️

In Jersey City, New Jersey, there's a factory that's been making pencils since 1889. Five generations. One of the last pencil factories in America.

"What if we took a pencil factory and made it save the planet?"

General Pencil Company already knows how to shape cedar into perfect hexagons, lacquer them in that iconic yellow, and ship them to hands that want to create something. We just asked them to point those pencils at a different kind of canvas — your teeth.

Same cedar. Same craft. Same American factory. But now, instead of writing on paper, you're writing a better future — one brush at a time.
