Wait — What’s a Miswak? 🦷

It’s the original toothbrush. Seriously. Humans have been cleaning their teeth with this thing for over 7,000 years. We just made it look like a No. 2 pencil.

Salvadora persica

The miswak (also called siwak or chewing stick) is a teeth-cleaning twig from the Salvadora persica tree — native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and South Asia.

The WHO recommends miswak as an effective tool for maintaining oral hygiene. Its fibers naturally contain silica (abrasive), fluoride (cavity protection), trimethylamine (antibacterial), and salvadorine (natural disinfectant).

No toothpaste required. No plastic. Just chew the tip until it’s frayed, brush.
